# Task 04 – Relative Uncertainty

## Problem Statement

A car's speedometer has a $5\%$ uncertainty. If the reading is $60\,\text{km/h}$, what is the range of the car's actual speed?

## Theory

Relative uncertainty is expressed as a percentage of the measured value. The absolute uncertainty $\Delta x$ is calculated from the relative uncertainty $\epsilon$ and the measured value $x$:

$$
\Delta x = x \cdot \epsilon
$$

The range of the measurement is given by:

$$
[x - \Delta x, x + \Delta x]
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify given values:**
   $x = 60\,\text{km/h}$
   $\epsilon = 5\% = 0.05$

2. **Calculate absolute uncertainty:**

$$
\Delta x = 60 \cdot 0.05 = 3\,\text{km/h}
$$

3. **Determine the range:**

$$
\text{Lower limit} = 60 - 3 = 57\,\text{km/h}
$$

$$
\text{Upper limit} = 60 + 3 = 63\,\text{km/h}
$$

## Final Result

The actual speed of the car is in the range of $57\,\text{km/h}$ to $63\,\text{km/h}$.

## Interpretation

A $5\%$ uncertainty implies that for every $100$ units measured, the instrument might be off by $5$ units. At $60\,\text{km/h}$, this margin of error is small enough to generally follow speed limits but significant enough for precision testing.
