# Task 05 – Percentage Calculation

## Problem Statement

A measurement of time is recorded as $t = 5.45 \pm 0.22$ seconds. Calculate the percentage uncertainty of this measurement.

## Theory

Percentage uncertainty (relative uncertainty expressed as a percent) is the ratio of the absolute uncertainty $\Delta x$ to the measured value $x$, multiplied by $100$:

$$
\epsilon_{\%} = \left( \frac{\Delta x}{x} \right) \cdot 100\%
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify values:**
   $t = 5.45\,\text{s}$
   $\Delta t = 0.22\,\text{s}$

2. **Calculate the ratio:**

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t} = \frac{0.22}{5.45} \approx 0.040367
$$

3. **Convert to percentage:**

$$
\epsilon_{\%} = 0.040367 \cdot 100\% \approx 4.04\%
$$

## Final Result

The percentage uncertainty of the measurement is $4.04\%$.

## Interpretation

The percentage uncertainty provides a sense of the quality of the measurement. A value of $4\%$ suggests a moderately precise measurement, typical of manual timing with a stopwatch where human reaction time is a factor.
