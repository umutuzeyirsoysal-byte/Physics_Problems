# Task 10 – Light Speed Measurement

## Problem Statement

Measure the speed of light using a microwave (f = 2.45 GHz) and chocolate. Calculate $c$ based on the distance between melted spots (wavelength) and find the percentage error compared to $3 \times 10^8\,\text{m/s}$.

## Theory

Microwaves in an oven form standing waves. Melted spots occur at antinodes. The distance between two consecutive melted spots is half a wavelength ($\lambda/2$). The wave equation is:

$$
c = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify the wavelength:**
   Assume the measured distance between spots is $d = 6.1\,\text{cm} = 0.061\,\text{m}$.

$$
\lambda = 2d = 0.122\,\text{m}
$$

2. **Calculate speed of light:**
   $f = 2.45\,\text{GHz} = 2.45 \times 10^9\,\text{Hz}$

$$
c_{calc} = (2.45 \times 10^9)(0.122) = 298,900,000\,\text{m/s}
$$

3. **Calculate Percentage Error:**

$$
\% \text{ Error} = \frac{|c_{accepted} - c_{calc}|}{c_{accepted}} \times 100
$$

$$
\% \text{ Error} = \frac{|3.00 \times 10^8 - 2.989 \times 10^8|}{3.00 \times 10^8} \times 100 \approx 0.37\%
$$

## Final Result

- Measured $c \approx 2.99 \times 10^8\,\text{m/s}$
- Percentage Error $\approx 0.37\%$

## Interpretation

This experiment provides a surprisingly accurate estimation of the speed of light using household items, demonstrating the direct relationship between wave frequency, wavelength, and velocity.
