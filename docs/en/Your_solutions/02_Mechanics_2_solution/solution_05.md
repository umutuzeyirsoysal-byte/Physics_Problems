# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision

## Problem Statement

A 70 kg runner moving at $3\ \text{m/s}$ jumps onto a 140 kg stationary cart. Determine the final speed of the cart with the runner. Check if kinetic energy is conserved in this collision and explain.

## Theory

When the runner jumps onto the cart and they move together, a perfectly inelastic collision occurs. In the absence of external horizontal forces, the horizontal linear momentum is conserved:

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = p_{\text{final}}
$$

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Kinetic energy before and after the collision can be computed using:

$$
E_k = \frac{1}{2} m v^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Part 1: Final Speed

Given variables:
* $m_1 = 70\ \text{kg}$
* $v_1 = 3\ \text{m/s}$
* $m_2 = 140\ \text{kg}$
* $v_2 = 0\ \text{m/s}$

Applying momentum conservation:

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140) v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1\ \text{m/s}
$$

### Part 2: Kinetic Energy Analysis

Initial kinetic energy ($E_{ki}$):

$$
E_{ki} = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} (70) (3)^2 = 35 \cdot 9 = 315\ \text{J}
$$

Final kinetic energy ($E_{kf}$):

$$
E_{kf} = \frac{1}{2} (m_1 + m_2) v_f^2 = \frac{1}{2} (210) (1)^2 = 105\ \text{J}
$$

Calculate the loss in kinetic energy:

$$
\Delta E_k = E_{kf} - E_{ki} = 105 - 315 = -210\ \text{J}
$$

## Final Result

The final speed of the cart with the runner is $1\ \text{m/s}$. Kinetic energy is **not** conserved; exactly $210\ \text{J}$ of mechanical energy is lost.

## Interpretation

Kinetic energy is never conserved in an inelastic collision. The "lost" energy is converted into other forms, such as thermal energy, sound, and the internal work required to deform materials or overcome friction as the runner's feet slide against the cart before coming to relative rest.
