# Task 08 – AC Voltage Equation

## Problem Statement

The current in an AC circuit is $I(t) = 2 \sin(120\pi t)$. Find the equation for the voltage $V(t)$ across a $50\,\Omega$ resistor.

## Theory

For a purely resistive circuit, the voltage and current are in phase. According to Ohm's Law for instantaneous values:

$$
V(t) = I(t) R
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify Given Values:**
   $I(t) = 2 \sin(120\pi t)$
   $R = 50\,\Omega$

2. **Apply Ohm's Law:**

$$
V(t) = [2 \sin(120\pi t)] \cdot 50
$$

3. **Simplify:**

$$
V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)
$$

## Final Result

The equation for the voltage is:

$$
V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)
$$

## Interpretation

The voltage has the same frequency ($60\,\text{Hz}$, since $\omega = 120\pi = 2\pi f$) as the current. The peak voltage is $100\,\text{V}$.
