# Task 13 – Transformer Ratio

## Problem Statement

A transformer steps down $120\,\text{V}$ AC to $9.0\,\text{V}$ AC. If the primary coil has $N_1 = 400$ turns, how many turns $N_2$ must the secondary coil have?

## Theory

The transformer turns ratio equation is:

$$
\frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{N_1}{N_2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Rearrange for $N_2$:**

$$
N_2 = N_1 \left( \frac{V_2}{V_1} \right)
$$

2. **Substitute values:**

$$
N_2 = 400 \left( \frac{9.0}{120} \right)
$$

3. **Simplify:**

$$
N_2 = 400 \cdot 0.075 = 30
$$

## Final Result

The secondary coil must have $30$ turns.

## Interpretation

To achieve a significant reduction in voltage (from $120\,\text{V}$ to $9\,\text{V}$), the number of turns in the secondary winding must be proportionally smaller than in the primary.
