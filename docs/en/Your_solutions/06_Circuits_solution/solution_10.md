# Task 10 – Average Current

## Problem Statement

A lightning bolt transfers $30\,\text{C}$ of charge to the ground in $2\,\text{ms}$. Calculate the average current.

## Theory

Average current $I_{avg}$ is the total charge $\Delta Q$ divided by the time interval $\Delta t$:

$$
I_{avg} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Convert units to SI:**
   $\Delta Q = 30\,\text{C}$
   $\Delta t = 2\,\text{ms} = 0.002\,\text{s}$

2. **Calculate Current:**

$$
I_{avg} = \frac{30}{0.002}
$$

$$
I_{avg} = 15,000\,\text{A} = 15\,\text{kA}
$$

## Final Result

The average current is $15,000\,\text{A}$ (or $15\,\text{kA}$).

## Interpretation

While the duration is extremely short, the massive amount of charge leads to a very high current, explaining the destructive power of lightning.
