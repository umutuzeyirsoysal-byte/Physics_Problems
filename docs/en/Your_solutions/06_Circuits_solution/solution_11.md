# Task 11 – Power & Energy

## Problem Statement

A $50\,\text{V}$ voltage is applied across a $100\,\Omega$ resistor. Calculate:
1. Power dissipated.
2. Energy consumed in $5$ minutes.

## Theory

Power $P$ in terms of voltage $V$ and resistance $R$:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

Energy $E$ is power multiplied by time $t$:

$$
E = P \cdot t
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Calculate Power:**

$$
P = \frac{50^2}{100} = \frac{2500}{100} = 25\,\text{W}
$$

2. **Calculate Time in seconds:**

$$
t = 5 \times 60 = 300\,\text{s}
$$

3. **Calculate Energy:**

$$
E = 25\,\text{W} \cdot 300\,\text{s} = 7500\,\text{J} = 7.5\,\text{kJ}
$$

## Final Result

- Power: $25\,\text{W}$
- Energy: $7.5\,\text{kJ}$

## Interpretation

The resistor converts electrical energy into thermal energy at a rate of $25$ Joules per second.
