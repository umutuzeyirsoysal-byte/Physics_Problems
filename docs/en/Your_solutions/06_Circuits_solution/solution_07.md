# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors, $C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a $10\,\text{V}$ battery. Calculate:
1. Total charge stored.
2. Total energy stored.

## Theory

For capacitors in parallel, the total capacitance $C_t$ is:

$$
C_t = C_1 + C_2
$$

The total charge $Q$ is:

$$
Q = C_t V
$$

The total energy $U$ is:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C_t V^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Calculate Total Capacitance:**

$$
C_t = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} = 10\,\mu\text{F} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}
$$

2. **Calculate Total Charge:**

$$
Q = (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F})(10\,\text{V}) = 100 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C} = 100\,\mu\text{C}
$$

3. **Calculate Total Energy:**

$$
\begin{align}
U &= \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F})(10\,\text{V})^2 \\
&= \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6})(100) \\
&= 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J} = 0.5\,\text{mJ}
\end{align}
$$

## Final Result

- Total Charge: $100\,\mu\text{C}$
- Total Energy: $0.5\,\text{mJ}$

## Interpretation

Connecting capacitors in parallel increases the total charge-storing capacity. Both capacitors experience the same potential difference ($10\,\text{V}$), but the $6\,\mu\text{F}$ capacitor stores more charge than the $4\,\mu\text{F}$ one.
