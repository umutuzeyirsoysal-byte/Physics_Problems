# Task 09 – Current

## Problem Statement

The charge flowing through a wire is given by $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$. Calculate the current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$.

## Theory

Electric current $I$ is defined as the instantaneous rate of flow of charge $Q$ with respect to time $t$:

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The charge function is:

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Differentiating with respect to $t$:

$$
\begin{align}
I(t) &= \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5) \\
&= 10t
\end{align}
$$

To find the current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$:

$$
I(3) = 10(3) = 30\,\text{A}
$$

## Final Result

The current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$ is $30\,\text{A}$.

## Interpretation

Since the charge $Q(t)$ increases quadratically with time, the current $I(t)$ increases linearly. This indicates a non-steady state flow where the rate of charge transport is accelerating.
