# Task 10 – Lorentz Force acting on Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire $2.0\text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10\text{ A}$ in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle $\theta$ between the wire and the field is:
a) $90^\circ$
b) $45^\circ$
c) $0^\circ$

## Theory

The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is:

$$
F = ILB\sin(\theta)
$$

Where:
* $I = 10\text{ A}$
* $L = 2.0\text{ m}$
* $B = 0.5\text{ T}$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Case (a): $\theta = 90^\circ$

$$
F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)\sin(90^\circ) = 10 \cdot 1 = 10\text{ N}
$$

### 2. Case (b): $\theta = 45^\circ$

$$
F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)\sin(45^\circ) = 10 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 7.07\text{ N}
$$

### 3. Case (c): $\theta = 0^\circ$

$$
F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)\sin(0^\circ) = 10 \cdot 0 = 0\text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

* a) $10\text{ N}$
* b) $7.07\text{ N}$
* c) $0\text{ N}$

## Interpretation
The magnetic force is maximal when the current flow is perpendicular to the field lines and vanishes completely when the current flows parallel to the field lines.
