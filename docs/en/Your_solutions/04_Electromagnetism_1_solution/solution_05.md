# Task 05 – Field Levitation

## Problem Statement

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface?

## Theory

For a particle to levitate, the upward electric force must equal the downward gravitational force:

$$
\vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_e + \vec{F}_g = 0
$$

The magnitudes must satisfy:

$$
qE = mg
$$

Where:
* $E$ is the electric field strength.
* $q$ is the charge of the proton ($1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$).
* $m$ is the mass of the proton ($1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$).
* $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity ($9.8\text{ m/s}^2$).

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Rearrange for E

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

### 2. Substitute Numerical Values

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg})(9.8\text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}}
$$

### 3. Calculate

$$
E \approx \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ V/m}
$$

## Final Result

The required electric field strength is approximately $1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ V/m}$ (or $\text{N/C}$), directed vertically upward.

## Interpretation

This is an extremely small electric field. It highlights how powerful the electromagnetic interaction is compared to gravity; even a very weak field is sufficient to counteract the entire gravitational pull of the Earth on a single proton.
